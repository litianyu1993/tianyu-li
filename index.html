AI Researcher

Tianyu Li

I am a senior research scientist at Samsung AI Center Montreal. I obtained my Ph.D. in Computer Science from McGill University/MILA (Quebec AI institute) under the supervision of Doina Precup and Guillaume Rabusseau. Before that, I graduated with a MSc in Computer Science from McGill University as well. I finished my Bachelor studies in both Computer Science and Statistics in Xiamen University, where I obtained BScs in Computer Science and Economics.

My research background predominantly revolves around sequence modeling, encompassing both theoretical aspects and practical applications. More recently, I am working on finding solutions for the hallucination problem in large language models.

Janurary 2023

Our paper Low-Rank Representation of Reinforcement Learning Policies is accepted to Journal of Artificial Intelligence Research (JAIR). In this paper we propose a general framework for policy representation for reinforcement learning tasks through a low-dimensional embedding of the policy on a reproducing kernel Hilbert space (RKHS).

August 2022

Our paper Traffic Scenario Clustering and Load Balancing with Distilled Reinforcement Learning Policies is accepted to IEEE International Conference on Communications. In this paper we focus on the load balancing problem of wireless communication traffic. We propose to construct a concise set of controllers that cover a wide range of traffic scenarios, allowing the operator to select a suitable controller for each sector based on local traffic conditions.

March 2022

Our paper Sequential Density Estimation via Nonlinear Continuous Weighted Finite Automata is accepted to LearnAut Workshop at EATCS International Colloquium on Automata, Languages and Programming (ICALP). In this paper we show a way for using weighted finite automata to approximate density functions by extending the WFA model to its nonlinear and continuous case.

March 2022

The extended version of our AISTATS 2019 paper Connecting Weighted Automata, Tensor Networks and Recurrent Neural Networks through Spectral Learning has been accepted for publication in the Machine Learning Journal. Compared to the previous conference paper, we further illustrate the connection between WFAs and tensor networks.
